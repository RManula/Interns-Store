# Civil & Infrastructure Engineering Internship - BHP

## Role Description
BHP's Engineering & Projects team manages the design, construction, and maintenance of critical infrastructure across its Western Australian iron ore operations — including rail lines stretching over 1,000 kilometres, processing plants, port facilities, and mine-site civil structures. As a Civil & Infrastructure Engineering intern, you'll support project delivery and asset integrity programs across these world-scale operations.

## Key Responsibilities
* Assist civil engineers in preparing design calculations, technical specifications, and drawings for mine site infrastructure projects
* Support asset integrity inspections and structural assessments of existing mine-site civil structures, roads, and embankments
* Contribute to project management documentation including schedule updates, cost tracking, and risk registers for active capital projects
* Conduct site surveys, geotechnical data collection, and environmental baseline measurements during site exposure rotations

## Technical Skills & Requirements
* Civil engineering fundamentals including structural analysis, geotechnics, hydrology, and earthworks design
* Proficiency in CAD software (AutoCAD, Civil 3D) and engineering design tools
* Understanding of construction management processes, safety-in-design principles, and major project delivery frameworks
* Enrolled in Civil Engineering, Structural Engineering, or Geotechnical Engineering (penultimate or final year; Engineers Australia accredited program preferred)

## Perks & Benefits
* Site rotation to BHP's Pilbara iron ore operations — see your engineering at work in the field
* FIFO support for Pilbara site rotations and Perth accommodation subsidy for interstate candidates
* Mentorship from BHP's Principal Engineers and project directors overseeing billion-dollar capital programs
* Pathway to BHP's Engineering Graduate Program and professional registration (CPEng) support track
